# Process Overview

This document describes the current consulting engagement workflow based on the initial stakeholder discovery session with Michael Reynolds (Managing Partner).

The documented process covers the lifecycle of a client engagement from lead generation through project delivery and billing. Since the discovery session provided only high-level information, this document distinguishes between confirmed information and areas that require further validation.

# Detailed Workflow

## Stage 1 – Lead Generation

### Objective

Receive a new client inquiry.

### Activities

- Client contacts the company through:
  - Referral
  - LinkedIn
  - Company Website
- Lead may be entered into HubSpot.
- Discovery call is scheduled.

### Output

Qualified discovery meeting.

---

## Stage 2 – Discovery & Qualification

### Objective

Understand the client's business needs and determine whether the opportunity is a good fit.

### Activities

- Conduct discovery call.
- Gather client requirements.
- Evaluate project fit.

### Decision Point

**Is the client a good fit?**

- Yes → Prepare Proposal
- No → Opportunity Closed

---

## Stage 3 – Proposal Management

### Activities

- Create proposal.
- Send proposal to client.
- Client reviews proposal.
- Revise proposal if requested.

### Decision Point

**Has the client approved the proposal?**

- Yes → Agreement
- Revision Required → Update proposal and resend.

---

## Stage 4 – Project Initiation

### Activities

- Client signs agreement.
- Schedule kickoff meeting.
- Create project in Asana.
- Create shared project folder.

### Known Exception

Consultants sometimes begin project work before project setup is completed because clients want to move quickly.

---

## Stage 5 – Resource Allocation

### Activities

- Assign consultants.
- Review consultant availability.
- Review consultant expertise.

### Current Process

- Managed through spreadsheets.
- High-priority projects may be assigned directly by the Managing Partner.

---

## Stage 6 – Project Delivery

### Activities

- Consultants complete assigned tasks.
- Update Asana weekly.
- Collaborate using Slack and Email.

### Known Issue

Some consultants update Asana regularly.

Others provide updates only through Slack or Email.

---

## Stage 7 – Client Communication

Clients receive updates through one of the following methods:

- Weekly Status Meetings
- Written Reports
- Quick Calls

The communication method depends on client preference.

---

## Stage 8 – Billing

### Fixed Fee Projects

- Monthly invoices generated.

### Hourly Projects

- Consultants submit time entries.
- Accounting generates invoices.

### Known Issue

Late time entries delay billing.

---

## Stage 9 – Project Completion

### Current Information

The transcript confirms that projects are completed after delivery and billing.

### Unknown

No formal project closure process was discussed.

# Known Exceptions

- Some referral leads are not immediately entered into HubSpot.
- Proposal formats vary between consultants.
- Consultants occasionally begin work before project setup.
- Resource allocation is sometimes performed directly by the Managing Partner.
- Project updates are not always entered into Asana.
- Documentation may be stored in Asana, Google Drive, or Email.
- Late consultant time entries delay invoice generation.

# Assumptions

The following assumptions were made while documenting the process and should be validated with stakeholders:

- Every project follows the same high-level lifecycle.
- Every project has a Project Manager.
- Every client signs an agreement before work begins (except identified exceptions).
- Weekly project updates are expected from all consultants.
- Billing begins after project initiation.
- Projects are formally closed after delivery.

---

# Conclusion

The current consulting delivery process provides a clear high-level workflow but lacks standardization across several stages. Variations in proposal creation, project setup, documentation, project tracking, and billing create inconsistencies and reduce operational visibility.

Standardizing these activities through documented processes, clearly defined ownership, and consistent use of systems would improve project delivery, collaboration, and client experience.
